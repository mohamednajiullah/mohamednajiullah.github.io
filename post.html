Moto G4 Plus Review

Design Disappoints

Posted by Ramees Raja on June 26, 2016

Before starting the review, I thank Varun Krish, CEO FoneArena, for letting me use this mobile, spl thanks to Siraj for lending me a helping hand.

Pros:

  • Stock OS

Cons:

  • Design
  • Non-Removeable battery

Specifications

The phone packs Qualcomm MSM8952 Snapdragon chipset with 1.5 GHz Cortex-A53 processor Quad-Core & 1.2 GHz Cortex-A53 Quad-Core, 32 GB ROM and 3 GB RAM or 16/2GB model. 128 GB dedicated micro SD expansion slot is also available. Here I use a 32/3GB model.

5.5‚Äù In-plane Switching display, packing 401 ppi and some good viewing angles and a slighter cooler tint is much visible. Corning Gorilla Glass 3 will take care of the screen protection area.

Below average build quality and bad design

Build Quality & Design

Below average build quality and bad design, something which I never expected from Moto G series. The phone looks more of plastic and never felt premium in any case. The bumped-up camera unit, plain back, ugly looking fingerprint scanner, an enormous letdown.

A Break from the past - Unsuccessful!!

The device is comparatively lighter than Mote G3. The fingerprint reader speed is average. 3000 mAh battery and turbocharging feature, should do some justice for a mid-range smartphone.

Connectivity

4G LTE, 3G, Bluetooth 4.0 & micro USB comes as a typical connectivity package with this phone. Mobile even supports OTG.

Camera

The camera is great, with its 16MP rear cam & 5 MP wide angle front cam, selfies can now cover a bigger crowd too. Now Camera has a manual mode which was missed in the earlier G series, a welcome move. HDR pic is also good, though. Camera does a spectacular job when clicking photos in a low-light condition.

Great color reproduction Image Stabilization is a bit shaky!!

Usage

Unit comes with Android M stock OS, with slide-down app drawer. Bigger Icon looks quite weird, better to opt for launchers. No heating issues were reported till date; gaming is good; internet browsing is also fair enough.

Pet Peeves & Verdict

The thing that bothers me now is the design. Also, this phone has a fierce competitor in the same range. A decent deal to bag. I'm disappointed, a Moto G3 user.

My Rating: 3.75/5

Note: Lenovo Zuk Z1 vs Moto G4 Plus, heads on, coming soon. Watch out!!
